Have questions about how Camila handles releases, communicates changes, or manages breaking API updates? You'll find answers to the most common questions below.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How will I know when a new version ships?">
    We communicate every release through multiple channels so nothing catches you off guard:

    - **This page** — The Product Updates tab is always the first place to check. It's updated on or before the release date for every major and minor version.
    - **Email announcements** — We send email notifications to all workspace admins for every major and minor release. Emails include a summary of what changed, any action required, and a link to the full changelog.
    - **In-app notifications** — If a release contains breaking changes or requires action from your team, you'll see a banner notification inside Camila when you next log in. These notifications link directly to the relevant migration guide.

    Patch releases (bug fixes and security updates) are typically deployed silently unless they fix a user-facing issue, in which case they'll appear in the Changelog.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I need to do anything when a new version releases?">
    It depends on the type of release:

    - **Patch releases (x.x.1, x.x.2, etc.)** — No action needed. These are deployed automatically and are fully backward-compatible. You may notice bugs fixed or small improvements, but nothing in your workflow will break.
    - **Minor releases (x.1.0, x.2.0, etc.)** — No action needed. Minor releases are always backward-compatible. New features are opt-in where possible. You can adopt new capabilities at your own pace.
    - **Major releases (2.0, 3.0, etc.)** — Action may be required if your team uses the Camila API directly or relies on specific platform behaviors that have changed. We will communicate any required migration steps with **at least 4 weeks' notice** before a major release goes live, so your team has time to prepare.

    When in doubt, check the Major Releases page and the Changelog for the specific version you're moving to.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What counts as a breaking change?">
    We define a breaking change as any modification that could cause an existing, correctly implemented integration or workflow to stop working without any changes on your end. Specifically, this includes:

    - **Removing or renaming an API field** — For example, renaming `data.items` to `data.results` (as in the v2.0 release).
    - **Changing or removing an endpoint URL** — Moving an endpoint to a new path or deprecating it entirely.
    - **Modifying default behavior** — Any change where the default output or behavior of a feature changes in a way that could break an integration relying on the previous default.
    - **Changing authentication requirements** — Any update to how tokens, signatures, or session credentials are validated.

    Changes that are **not** considered breaking include: adding new optional fields to API responses, adding new endpoints, improving error messages, and performance improvements.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How long do you support older API versions?">
    We support the previous major API version for **at least 12 months** after a new major version ships. During this period, the older API version continues to function normally and receives security patches.

    For example, when Camila 2.0 shipped in December 2024, the v1 API entered a deprecation period and will remain active until at least December 2025. After that point, it may be removed with appropriate notice.

    Deprecation notices always appear in the Changelog with the specific sunset date. We will also send direct email reminders to any workspace that has active traffic on a deprecated API version in the 90, 30, and 7 days leading up to the sunset date.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Where do I report a bug?">
    We want to hear about bugs as quickly as possible so we can fix them for everyone. Here's how to report one:

    - **In-app feedback button** — Click the **"Send Feedback"** button in the bottom-left corner of any Camila page. This is the fastest way to reach us and automatically includes your session context.
    - **Email** — Send a report to [bugs@camila.app](mailto:bugs@camila.app) for issues you'd prefer to communicate in more detail.

    For either method, please include:
    - Your **workspace ID** (found in Settings → General)
    - **Steps to reproduce** the issue
    - **Expected behavior** vs. **actual behavior**
    - Screenshots or a screen recording if the issue is visual

    The more detail you provide, the faster our engineering team can diagnose and fix the problem.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How can I give feedback on the roadmap?">
    Community feedback directly shapes what we build next — it's not just a formality. Here's how to make your voice heard:

    - **Feedback portal** — Visit [feedback.camila.app](https://feedback.camila.app) to browse ideas already submitted by the community, upvote the ones that matter most to you, and submit your own feature requests.
    - **Contact the team** — Reach out via the [Contact](/updates/contact) page for more detailed conversations about specific needs, especially if you're an Enterprise customer with complex requirements.
    - **Roadmap page** — The [Roadmap](/updates/roadmap) page shows what's actively in progress, what's planned, and what we're considering. Items on the roadmap reflect a combination of community feedback, strategic priorities, and customer conversations.

    The product team reviews all submissions and upvotes regularly. High-impact, widely-requested ideas get surfaced directly to the product leadership team.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Are updates rolled out to all users at once?">
    Not always. We use progressive rollouts to reduce risk and catch unexpected issues before they affect everyone:

    - **Minor and patch releases** are progressively rolled out over **24–48 hours**. A small percentage of workspaces receive the update first, and we monitor error rates and performance before expanding to 100% of users.
    - **Major releases** are made available on a **set date announced in advance**. Because major releases may require action from your team, we don't push them silently — workspace admins are notified and the release is available from a specific date.

    During a progressive rollout, you might notice that a feature mentioned in the changelog isn't visible yet in your workspace. This is normal — it means your workspace is in a later cohort. You'll receive the update within the 24–48 hour window.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does Camila have a status page?">
    Yes. Visit **[status.camila.app](https://status.camila.app)** for:

    - **Real-time service status** across all Camila components (API, dashboard, file storage, webhooks, integrations)
    - **Active incidents** with live updates from the engineering team
    - **Incident history** so you can look up past outages and read post-mortems
    - **Uptime metrics** over the past 90 days

    You can subscribe to status updates via email or RSS so you're notified immediately when an incident is opened or resolved — without needing to check the page manually.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
