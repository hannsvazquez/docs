## June 2025 — Bulk Export & Dashboard Filtering are live

Two of our most-requested Q2 2025 features have officially landed. **Bulk Export** lets you pull thousands of records in a single action — no more paginating through the UI or stitching together manual downloads. Export to CSV or JSON on your schedule and plug your data directly into the tools your team already uses. **Advanced Dashboard Filtering** takes your analytics further with multi-condition filter logic, saved filter sets, and shareable filtered views, so every stakeholder can see exactly the slice of data they need. Together, these features are designed to save you time, help your team work faster, and put actionable insights at scale within everyone's reach.

## March 2025 — Team Workspaces launch

Your whole team, finally in one place. **Team Workspaces** makes it easy to invite everyone in your organization to Camila, assign roles and permissions, and collaborate in a shared environment built around the way your teams actually work. No more siloed accounts or shared logins — each member gets their own access, with the right level of visibility and control. Whether you're onboarding a department of five or rolling Camila out org-wide, Team Workspaces gives you the structure to do it right.

## December 2024 — Camila 2.0 is here

This is the one we've been building toward. **Camila 2.0** is our most significant release to date — a milestone that redefines what the platform can do. At its core is a fully unified API that brings every Camila resource under a consistent, predictable interface. Real-time collaboration is now native across the entire product, meaning your team can work together simultaneously without conflicts or stale data. We've also shipped massive performance improvements across the board: faster load times, snappier interactions, and infrastructure built to scale with your organization. Camila 2.0 isn't an incremental update — it's the foundation for everything we're building next.

<Warning>
  Camila 2.0 includes breaking changes to the API. If you use the Camila API, review the migration guide before upgrading.
</Warning>
